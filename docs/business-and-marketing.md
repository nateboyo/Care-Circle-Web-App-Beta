# Business And Marketing Plan

## Market Signal

AARP and the National Alliance for Caregiving reported in 2025 that 63 million Americans are family caregivers, nearly one in four adults. AARP's 2026 valuation report estimated that adult family caregivers provide about $1.01 trillion in unpaid care annually.

Sources:

- https://www.aarp.org/pri/topics/ltss/family-caregiving/caregiving-in-the-us-2025/
- https://www.aarp.org/press/releases/2026-03-26-AARP-Economic-Value-Of-Family-Caregiving-Report/

## Ideal First Customer

Primary buyer:

> Adult children, often working full-time, coordinating care for an aging parent with siblings or relatives.

Best early niche:

- Families managing two or more helpers
- Families dealing with frequent appointments
- Families with insurance calls and paperwork
- Families transitioning from hospital to home
- Families hiring their first home-care helper

Do not sell the first version to nursing homes. That path adds procurement, staff training, integrations, and heavier compliance work before you have proof that families want the product.

## Core Promise

CareCircle should not market itself as a generic family chat app.

Lead with:

> Stop losing care details in group texts.

Solo-dev beta positioning:

> A private family workspace for elder-care tasks, medication reminders, appointments, notes, insurance calls, and emergency handoffs.

Supporting promises:

- Everyone knows what happened today
- Meds, appointments, documents, and calls stay together
- The next caregiver can walk in prepared
- Emergency information can be exported quickly

## Pricing Strategy

Start simple:

- Free: basic coordination
- Family: $12/month
- Family Plus: $24/month
- Care Pro: $79/month

Annual plans:

- Family: $99/year
- Family Plus: $199/year
- Care Pro: $749/year

The strongest paid feature is the care packet export because it turns organization into something families can immediately use.

For beta, keep document storage lightweight: store document names and locations, not uploaded files. Paid conversion should come from care packet export, not storage volume.

## Acquisition Channels

Start with channels where caregivers already ask for help:

- Facebook caregiver groups
- Reddit caregiving communities
- Local senior centers
- Hospital discharge planner resource lists
- Elder-law offices
- Geriatric care managers
- Senior move managers
- Home-care agencies
- Pharmacies and physical therapy clinics
- SEO pages for practical searches like "caregiver checklist for siblings" and "elder care document organizer"

## Launch Campaign

Build three free lead magnets:

- Elder care emergency packet checklist
- Doctor visit prep checklist
- Insurance call log template

Each template should naturally point to the paid app:

> Want this shared with your family and updated automatically? Start a CareCircle.

## Partnership Plan

Best first partners:

- Elder-law attorneys
- Geriatric care managers
- Home-care agencies
- Senior move managers

Offer:

- Free partner directory listing
- 20 percent referral fee for the first year
- Co-branded care packet templates
- Care Pro account for managing multiple families

## Metrics To Watch

Activation:

- Family creates first care profile
- At least two members invited
- First medication or task added
- First appointment or document added

Conversion:

- Export care packet clicked
- Third document added
- Second family member joins
- First insurance call logged

Retention:

- Weekly active families
- Number of updates per family per week
- Tasks completed
- Medication reminders checked
- Messages sent

## First 90 Days

Days 1-30:

- Interview 20 caregivers
- Launch landing page and waitlist
- Offer free checklist downloads
- Manually onboard five families

Days 31-60:

- Add Stripe Checkout
- Add invite links
- Add encrypted file storage
- Start referral outreach to care managers and elder-law offices

Days 61-90:

- Launch paid Family plan
- Publish SEO content weekly
- Pilot Care Pro with three professionals
- Measure whether care packet export converts
